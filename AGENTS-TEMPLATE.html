You are one of many parallel sessions FLOOR

Seven to eight developers on YOUR team are driving agents against your team's Linear board (one board per team — the other team has its own). You coordinate through the board, not by guessing. The human speaks English; you speak swb — every board write goes through an swb verb, never a raw Linear MCP write. Verbal briefings die at the first context compaction; this file plus the hooks IS the protocol.

The MUSTs — the coordination floor FLOOR

  1. Claim before you edit. Never touch a file for a ticket you haven't claimed: swb claim <KEY> --files "<globs>" first — it assigns your human, moves to In Progress, cuts the worktree, declares your files. Work inside that worktree.
  2. Declare your files honestly. Your --files globs are what the ownership guard checks for everyone else. Under-declaring hides collisions; over-declaring blocks teammates.
  3. Never create Todo work. swb new lands in Backlog; only a human promotes. Never move a ticket to Todo yourself.
  4. Never close your own ticket. swb done → In Review is as far as you go. A human merges to Done.
  5. done is gated — don't fight it. It runs the team's tests first and refuses on failure, then requires a real --pr. Fix the tests or open the PR; never route around.
  6. Gate 2 survives skip-permissions. claim/done are DENIED by a hook without --approved. Hitting the denial means: ask your human in chat, get their explicit yes, re-run with --approved. NEVER add the flag without that yes — it's logged and visible forever.
  7. Read the full ticket before building. claim prints the spec; for any other ticket a digest references, swb show <KEY> first. Titles are headlines, not specs.
  8. Ask instead of guessing. Unknown you'd otherwise guess at? swb ask <KEY> @<owner> "…", park that piece, keep working elsewhere. Never block, never guess.
  9. No drive-by refactors. Nothing outside your declared files, however ugly the neighbors. Cleanup is its own Backlog ticket.
  10. One claim at a time (WIP = 1). Finish or release before claiming the next. TEAM CHOICE teams may change the limit — the default is 1.
  11. Every write is signed; the human is accountable. 🤖 Claude — via <human> is appended automatically. Never impersonate a human answer.
  12. Subagents never write to the board. Fleets may read (sync/show/board/members); all writes come from the main session. One claim = one worktree, no matter how many subagents inside.
  13. Compose with the repo's own CLAUDE.md. bistrainerdev's hard stops, /bis-plan→/bis-build, design-system rules, dev-DB read-only all still bind. Conflict → the repo hard-stop wins.
  14. Use the knowledge tools before guessing at CFML. Codegraph MCP + BIS knowledge base answer codebase facts; LLM training data on ColdFusion is thin — verify idioms against the team's anchor files instead of inventing them.

Act on the digest — mandatory, not a bulletin FLOOR

Every prompt carries a board digest ending in an act directive. If any item — a claim, state change, discovery, @you — intersects your claimed ticket or declared files, stop and state the impact before continuing. "Session A changed the schema, so I'm adapting" is the behavior this whole system exists to produce; silently building on a stale assumption is the failure it exists to prevent.

Deep reads: reader subagents FLOOR

When multiple tickets need a deep read, don't swallow them into your own context — spawn a read-only subagent: "run swb show HAC-23 and HAC-31; report ONLY what affects src/player/**; two paragraphs." Readers are read-only; the PM runs the standing hourly version (stuck tickets, aging @asks, scope drift).
